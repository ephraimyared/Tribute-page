Billy Graham

American Evangelist, an ordained Southern Baptist minister.

Here is a life time of Billy Graham

  • 1918- Born in Charlotte, North Carolina
  • 1943- he attended his education
    B.A., Wheaton College—Wheaton, Illinois
    1940 Graduated, Florida Bible Institute (now Trinity College of Florida)
  • 1948- Graham was 29 when he became president of Northwestern Bible College in Minneapolis.
  • 1950-Founder and chairman, Billy Graham Evangelistic Association. He also Began Hour of Decision weekly radio program, broadcast around the world for more than 60 years.
  • 1947–52- President, Northwestern Schools in Minneapolis
  • Preaching- Billy Graham preached the Gospel of Jesus Christ to some 215 million people who attended one of his more than 400 Crusades, simulcasts and evangelistic rallies in more than 185 countries and territories. A 2005 Gallup poll revealed that 35 million people in America—one in six U.S. adults—had heard Billy Graham preach in person.
  • Television Ministry- Regular prime-time broadcasts, which began in 1957, have reached millions of households in the U.S. and around the world. In 2013, Graham delivered his final message—the culmination of his My Hope America project—via more than 480 stations.
  • Published 34 books (1947–2017) "My Answer" (since 1952)—syndicated column carried by newspapers throughout the U.S. with a combined circulation of some 5 million.

  • Graham was considered "among the most influential Christian leaders" of the 20th century.
  • Graham said that his planned retirement was a result of his failing health; he had suffered from hydrocephalus from 1992 on. In August 2005, Graham appeared at the groundbreaking for his library in Charlotte, North Carolina. Then 86, he used a walker during the ceremony. On July 9, 2006, he spoke at the Metro Maryland Franklin Graham Festival , held in Baltimore, Maryland at Oriole Park at Camden Yards. In April 2010, Graham was 91 and experiencing substantial vision, hearing, and balance loss when he made a rare public appearance at the re-dedication of the renovated Billy Graham Library.
  • Died Feb. 21, 2018 Montreat, North Carolina.
